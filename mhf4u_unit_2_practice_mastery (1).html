Q1: The Efficiency Test Level 1 - Warm Up

Find the remainder when the polynomial is divided by the binomial:
P(x) = x3 - 2x2 + 4x - 5
divided by (x - 2)
Before calculating: You have three methods available. Long division, synthetic division, or the Remainder Theorem. Which one is the fastest when you only need the remainder?
Hint: The Remainder Theorem states that the remainder of P(x) ÷ (x - k) is exactly equal to P(k). What is your 'k' value here?
Step 1: Identify k
The divisor is (x - 2), so k = 2.
Step 2: Evaluate P(2)
P(2) = (2)3 - 2(2)2 + 4(2) - 5
P(2) = 8 - 2(4) + 8 - 5
P(2) = 8 - 8 + 8 - 5
Final Answer: The remainder is 3.
Ontario Exam Trap: Many students waste 3-4 minutes doing full long division for this 1-mark question. Time management is key. If a question only asks for the remainder, always use the Remainder Theorem!

Q2: Working Backwards Level 2 - Moderate

Determine the value of k so that the polynomial leaves a remainder of 7 when divided by (x + 1).
P(x) = kx3 + 5x2 - 2x + 3
Logic Check: We know the remainder is 7. How can we set up an algebraic equation using the Remainder Theorem to isolate k?
Hint 1: The divisor is (x + 1), which means you need to evaluate P(-1). Set the entire equation equal to the known remainder: P(-1) = 7.
Step 1: Set up the equation
P(-1) = 7
k(-1)3 + 5(-1)2 - 2(-1) + 3 = 7
Step 2: Simplify and isolate k
k(-1) + 5(1) + 2 + 3 = 7
-k + 5 + 5 = 7
-k + 10 = 7
-k = -3
Final Answer: k = 3.
Extension Question: Now that you know k = 3, is (x - 1) a factor of this polynomial?

(Mentally calculate P(1) with k=3 to check if remainder is 0!)

Q3: The Disguised Polynomial Level 3 - Advanced

Fully factor the following polynomial over the real numbers:
f(x) = x4 - 5x2 + 4
Strategic Choice: This is a degree 4 polynomial. You could use the Factor Theorem to find P(1)=0, then do synthetic division twice. BUT, look closely at the exponents (4 and 2). Is there a faster Grade 10 method hiding here?
Hint 1: This is a "Quadratic in Disguise". Let m = x2. Then m2 = x4. Rewrite the equation in terms of m.
Step 1: Substitution (Quadratic form)
Let m = x2. The expression becomes:
m2 - 5m + 4
Step 2: Factor the simple quadratic
Find two numbers that multiply to 4 and add to -5. (-4 and -1).
(m - 4)(m - 1)
Step 3: Substitute back and factor fully
Replace m with x2:
(x2 - 4)(x2 - 1)

Both are Difference of Squares!
f(x) = (x - 2)(x + 2)(x - 1)(x + 1)

Q4: Spot the Mistake Error Analysis

A student used synthetic division to divide:

(x3 - 27) ÷ (x - 3)
3 | 1 -27
| 3
--------
1 -24

Quotient: x - 24, Remainder: 0
Analyze the setup: The student put a '3' outside the bracket, which is correct for (x-3). They wrote '1' for the x3 term and '-27' for the constant. What critical rule of polynomial division did they break?
The Mistake:
They forgot the placeholder zeros for missing degrees! The polynomial is actually x3 + 0x2 + 0x - 27.
Correct Setup & Solution:

3 | 1 0 0 -27 | 3 9 27 ----------------- 1 3 9 | 0

Quotient: x2 + 3x + 9

Q5: The Systems Challenge Level 4 - Challenge

When P(x) = ax3 - x2 + 2x + b is divided by (x - 1), the remainder is 10.

When it is divided by (x - 2), the remainder is 51.

Determine the values of a and b.
Logic Check: We have TWO unknown variables (a and b). In algebra, to solve for two unknowns, you need a system of TWO equations. How can the Remainder Theorem give you two distinct equations?
Hint 1: Create Equation 1
Using the first condition: P(1) = 10.
a(1)3 - (1)2 + 2(1) + b = 10
Simplify this to get an equation in terms of a and b.
Hint 2: Create Equation 2
Using the second condition: P(2) = 51.
a(2)3 - (2)2 + 2(2) + b = 51
Simplify this. Then use substitution or elimination with Equation 1!
Eq 1: a(1) - 1 + 2 + b = 10  ⇒  a + b = 9 (Eq 1)
Eq 2: a(8) - 4 + 4 + b = 51  ⇒  8a + b = 51 (Eq 2)
Step 3: Elimination
Subtract Eq 1 from Eq 2:
(8a + b) - (a + b) = 51 - 9
7a = 42
a = 6
Step 4: Solve for b
Sub 'a' into Eq 1: 6 + b = 9  ⇒  b = 3

Q6: The Ultimate Test Level 5 - Master Challenge

Analyze the function: f(x) = -x3 + 3x2 - 4
  • a) Use Factor Theorem to fully factor.
  • b) State zeros and multiplicity.
  • c) State end behaviour.
  • d) Sketch the graph.
Test factors of -4: (±1, ±2, ±4)
f(-1) = -(-1)3 + 3(-1)2 - 4 = 1 + 3 - 4 = 0.
Therefore, (x + 1) is a factor.
Synthetic Division by (x+1):
Use root -1. Note the missing 'x' term! (0x).
Quotient is: -x2 + 4x - 4
Factor the quadratic:
-(x2 - 4x + 4)  ⇒  -(x - 2)(x - 2)
f(x) = -(x + 1)(x - 2)2
Based on f(x) = -(x + 1)(x - 2)2:
  • x = -1 (Multiplicity 1)  ⇒  Crosses the axis.
  • x = 2 (Multiplicity 2)  ⇒  Bounces off the axis.
  • y-intercept: f(0) = -4.
End Behaviour: Degree 3 (Odd), Leading Coeff is Negative. Trend is down. Starts Q2, Ends Q4.

Mission Accomplished

Key Takeaways

  • Remainder Theorem is faster than division for finding remainders.
  • Always check for missing placeholders ($0x^2$) in synthetic division.
  • For multi-variable problems ($a$ and $b$), set up a linear system.
  • Don't forget to common factor before using complex theorems!
